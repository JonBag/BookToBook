Soil Types for Plants

Loam Soil

Loam soil is a balanced mixture of sand, silt, and clay. It is considered the ideal soil for most plants because it retains moisture and nutrients well while allowing excess water to drain away.

Clay Soil

Clay soil is rich in nutrients but can be heavy and compacted. It retains water well but can become waterlogged. Adding organic matter can improve its structure.

Sandy Soil

Sandy soil has large particles and drains quickly. It is low in nutrients and doesn't retain moisture well. Adding organic matter can help improve its fertility.

Silty Soil

Silty soil has fine particles and retains moisture well. It is fertile and good for growing a variety of plants, but it can become compacted easily.
